Case Studies/Maritime Incident & CRM Platform
Dove365 Project

Maritime Incident & CRM Platform
for Global Spill Response

A global maritime environmental response organisation needed a connected platform for CRM, incident response, commitments, claims, safety reporting, AI-assisted assessment, and live operational analytics.

Sector Maritime environmental response
Engagement Dove365 project
Platform Power Platform, Azure & AI
Scale Global operations
The Challenge

The problem behind the work

The organisation operated across live spill response, technical advisory work, claims support, training, and global stakeholder engagement. Contacts, incidents, staff commitments, claims, safety records, and documents were handled across separate systems and spreadsheets.

This made it difficult to see which staff were deployed, which contacts were linked to an incident, what commitments had been made, which claims were active, and where evidence was stored.

Disconnected CRM and incident data Contacts, accounts, insurers, vessels, and incidents were not reliably connected in one operational view.
Informal commitments tracking Staff deployments, conferences, and operational engagements lacked a central record of who was where and why.
Claims outside the core system Incident-related claims were tracked through emails and spreadsheets, limiting auditability and follow-up.
Documents detached from context Photos, evidence, and assessments were stored externally without clear links back to the incident record.
Manual reporting Operational dashboards and reports required manual extraction and preparation.
The Solution

What was delivered

A suite of interconnected model-driven applications was delivered on a shared Dataverse architecture, with AI Builder, Power Automate, Power BI, and controlled ALM practices built in.

CRM foundation

Contacts, accounts, insurers, shipping organisations, and stakeholder relationships were centralised and linked to incidents and commitments.

Incident management app

Incident records captured vessel details, substance tracking, location, response activity, linked people, documents, dashboards, and reports.

Commitments tracker

Staff deployments and engagements were tracked as structured records linked back to CRM and incident context.

Claims management

Claims were created and managed against incident records, with auditable status history and relevant relationships.

Safety reporting

Accidents and near misses were captured in a governed internal reporting app for follow-up and trend visibility.

AI and analytics

AI Builder supported spill image assessment and business card capture, while embedded Power BI delivered live operational reporting.

Outcomes

What changed

Connected operations

CRM, incidents, commitments, claims, and documents now flow through one shared operational data model.

AI-assisted assessment

AI Builder gives technical teams a faster starting point when reviewing incident images and response options.

Cleaner contact capture

Business card scanning reduces manual entry after conferences, meetings, and response activity.

Incident-context documents

Evidence and documents are easier to retrieve because they are linked from the operational record.

Live insight

Embedded dashboards and paginated reports provide current operational and claims visibility.

Future-ready architecture

Layered solutions and Dev, UAT, and production pipelines support controlled future enhancement.
